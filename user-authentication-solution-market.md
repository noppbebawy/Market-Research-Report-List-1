<p><ul><li>Single Factor Authentication</li><li>Two Factor Authentication</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The user authentication solution market is divided into two primary types: Single Factor Authentication (SFA) and Two Factor Authentication (2FA). Single Factor Authentication relies on one verification method, usually a password, making it simple but less secure. In contrast, Two Factor Authentication enhances security by requiring two forms of verification, such as a password plus a biometric scan or a one-time code sent to a mobile device. This layered approach significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access, addressing growing security concerns.</p></p>
