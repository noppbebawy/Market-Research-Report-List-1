<p><p>Laboratory cell sorting equipment encompasses various technologies for isolating specific cell populations. Fluorescent Activated Cell Sorting (FACS) uses fluorescence to identify and sort cells based on specific markers. Magnetic-Activated Cell Sorting (MACS) employs magnetic beads to facilitate the separation of targeted cells from a heterogeneous mixture. MEMS-based microfluidic devices offer precise manipulation of cells in small volumes, enabling high-throughput sorting with minimal sample loss. Together, these technologies enhance research and clinical applications in cell biology and therapeutics.</p></p>
