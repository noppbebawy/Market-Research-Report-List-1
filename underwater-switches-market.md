<p><p>The underwater switches market comprises limit switches and proximity switches designed for use in aquatic environments. Limit switches are used to detect the presence or absence of an object, ensuring safety and operational control in underwater machinery. Proximity switches, on the other hand, utilize electromagnetic fields to sense nearby objects without physical contact, making them ideal for harsh underwater conditions. Both types enhance automation and reliability in marine applications, supporting industries like underwater exploration, aquaculture, and subaquatic robotics.</p></p>
