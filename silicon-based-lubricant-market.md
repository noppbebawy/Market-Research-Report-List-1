<p><p>The silicon-based lubricant market is categorized into "dry" and "wet" types, each serving distinct applications. Dry lubricants, often powdered or in spray form, are ideal for high-temperature environments where traditional oils may break down; they leave minimal residue and provide low-friction surfaces. Wet lubricants, typically in liquid form, offer superior protection and lubrication, making them suitable for moving parts that operate in moisture-prone areas. Both types are essential across industries for enhancing performance and reducing wear.</p></p>
